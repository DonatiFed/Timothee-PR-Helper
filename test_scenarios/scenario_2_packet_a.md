# Packet A — The tweet resurfaces

**Talent:** Marcus Bell
**Scenario time:** 8:00 PM

Use this file with `challenge_brief.md`. This packet contains the only verified inputs available at 8:00 PM.

## Situation

A screenshot of a since-deleted tweet from three years ago started circulating at about 7:00 PM across X, Instagram, and Reddit. By 8:00 PM it has become a fast-moving controversy, with cancel-culture discourse amplifying the story hours before a scheduled live TV appearance on a late-night show.

Marcus is also in a long-term endorsement deal with **Apex Athletics**, a fictional sportswear brand, with a new campaign set to launch in 48 hours.

## Timing and approvals

- Marcus is in makeup and pre-interview prep until **8:35 PM**.
- Any public statement in Marcus's voice requires **Marcus's approval**.
- The manager can approve **sponsor and reporter holding replies** before then.
- Apex Athletics needs a sponsor-safe holding line by **8:20 PM**.
- That holding line must work before full facts are available and must be **40 words or fewer**.

## Viral clip

The screenshot shows a tweet attributed to Marcus reading: **"Classical music is just elevator music that pretentious people pretend to enjoy. Name one person under 40 who actually goes to the symphony."**

Known limits:
- the screenshot appears authentic and matches the account's formatting from that period
- the tweet has been deleted, no archive link is independently verified
- you do not know the conversational context (reply thread, prior tweets, tone)
- Marcus has not confirmed or denied authorship

**Status of that explanation:** internal context only. It is **not** independently verified by this packet.

## Talent profile

- Marcus is a popular athlete-turned-broadcaster with crossover appeal and a large social following.
- He is known for a lighthearted, everyman persona and rarely gets into public controversies.
- Because the tweet targets classical music, the backlash has spread into arts, culture, and education communities beyond his usual audience.

## Voice guide for outward-facing language

Aim for:
- warm, humble, and genuine
- specific without sounding like a legal statement
- respectful to musicians and the classical music community
- accountable for impact without confirming unverified tweet authorship

Do not:
- make **"it was just a joke"** the whole defense
- argue with critics or get combative
- try to make it a comedy bit before addressing the concern
- sound dismissive about classical music, orchestras, or arts education
- center late-night show excitement while backlash is active
