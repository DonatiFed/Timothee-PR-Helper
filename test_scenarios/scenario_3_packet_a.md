# Packet A — The photo drops

**Talent:** Camille Fontaine
**Scenario time:** 4:00 PM

Use this file with `challenge_brief.md`. This packet contains the only verified inputs available at 4:00 PM.

## Situation

A photo from a film set started circulating at about 3:10 PM across X, TikTok, Instagram, and entertainment news accounts. By 4:00 PM it has become a fast-moving backlash involving disability advocacy groups, with meme risk rising before a scheduled premiere and press junket tonight.

Camille is also the face of an ongoing campaign with **Lumière Parfums**, a fictional luxury fragrance brand, with a major premiere appearance tonight.

## Timing and approvals

- Camille is in a press junket interview rotation until **4:30 PM**.
- Any public statement in Camille's voice requires **Camille's approval**.
- The manager can approve **sponsor and reporter holding replies** before then.
- Lumière Parfums needs a sponsor-safe holding line by **4:15 PM**.
- That holding line must work before full facts are available and must be **40 words or fewer**.

## Viral clip

The photo shows Camille on set appearing to mimic a physical disability for comedic effect during a break between takes.

Known limits:
- the photo is real and was taken on an active film set
- it was taken during an informal moment, not during an actual scene
- the full context — including what was happening around her — is unknown
- the film's production company has not issued a statement

## Public signal snapshot

She is devastated that it's being read that way. Her instinct is to post a long Instagram story explaining exactly what happened. Premiere red carpet is at **7:00 PM**. Lumière is extremely worried.

**Status of that explanation:** internal context only. It is **not** independently verified by this packet.

## Talent profile

- Camille is a globally recognized actress with a massive social media following and multiple brand partnerships.
- She is known for a warm, approachable public persona and has previously supported inclusion initiatives.
- Because the photo involves apparent disability mockery, advocacy groups and accessibility organizations are leading the backlash, which gives it institutional weight beyond typical celebrity drama.

## Voice guide for outward-facing language

Aim for:
- compassionate, serious, and humble
- specific without sounding like it was written by a legal team
- respectful to the disability community
- accountable for impact without confirming or denying unverified context

Do not:
- make **"you're reading it wrong"** the whole message
- argue with advocacy groups or disability activists
- try to minimize the concern or call it an overreaction
- sound dismissive about disability representation or inclusion
- center premiere excitement while backlash is active
